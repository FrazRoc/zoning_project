About This Project

Mile High Potential is an interactive mapping tool that identifies over 2,500 parcels near RTD light rail stations with significant redevelopment potential. By visualizing opportunities for transit-oriented development (TOD), this project aims to support Denver's housing goals and promote sustainable, equitable growth.

2,521 Opportunity Parcels

~488K Potential Units

Why Transit-Oriented Development?

Transit-oriented development creates walkable, mixed-use communities around public transportation. Benefits include:

  • Reduced car dependency: Residents can walk, bike, or take transit to work and amenities
  • Lower housing costs: Reduced transportation expenses and increased housing supply
  • Environmental benefits: Lower carbon emissions and reduced urban sprawl
  • Economic vitality: Increased foot traffic supports local businesses
  • Housing equity: Access to opportunity for residents without cars

Opportunity Categories

The map identifies six types of redevelopment opportunities:

Large Vacant Land

218 parcels of vacant land over 0.5 acres, ideal for large-scale development projects.

Single-Family on Multi-Unit Zoning

673 parcels currently used for single-family homes but zoned for multi-unit housing.

Commercial on Mixed-Use Zoning

360 parcels with commercial buildings on mixed-use zoning allowing residential development.

Industrial Conversion

301 industrial properties on mixed-use zoning, ready for residential conversion.

Industrial Needs Rezoning

390 industrial parcels near transit that would require rezoning for residential use.

Teardown Candidates

579 underutilized properties with low improvement-to-land value ratios.

Methodology

Data Sources

  • Parcel Data: Denver Open Data (property boundaries, zoning, assessed values)
  • Zoning Districts: Denver Open Data (current zoning designations)
  • Transit Data: RTD GIS (light rail lines and station locations)

Selection Criteria

Parcels were selected based on:

  • Proximity to transit: Within 1/2 mile of RTD light rail stations
  • Zoning alignment: Currently zoned for residential or mixed-use development
  • Development potential: Underutilized land or buildings with redevelopment opportunity
  • Exclusions: Public land, parks, right-of-ways, existing apartment buildings, and narrow parcels (railroad corridors)

Unit Calculations

Potential housing units are estimated conservatively using each parcel's maximum allowed Floor Area Ratio (FAR) or story height from Denver's zoning code. We assume 1,500 gross square feet per unit, which accounts for:

  • Average unit size of ~900 sq ft (mix of studios, 1BR, 2BR)
  • Building efficiency of ~60% (common areas, lobbies, elevators, parking, mechanical)
Note: These are theoretical maximums based on zoning. Actual development would be constrained by economic feasibility, site conditions, parking requirements, historic preservation, and community input.

Limitations

This analysis has several important limitations:

  • Market feasibility: Not all opportunities are economically viable to develop
  • Site constraints: Flood zones, contamination, slopes, and other physical limitations are not considered
  • Owner intent: Property owners may not wish to sell or redevelop
  • Community impact: Development must balance growth with neighborhood character and displacement concerns
  • Infrastructure capacity: Water, sewer, and other utilities may require upgrades

How to Use This Map

  • Toggle layers: Use the layer control (top right) to show/hide different categories
  • Click parcels: View detailed information including zoning, size, and development potential
  • View transit context: See how parcels relate to rail lines and station buffers
  • Explore neighborhoods: Zoom and pan to examine different parts of Denver
